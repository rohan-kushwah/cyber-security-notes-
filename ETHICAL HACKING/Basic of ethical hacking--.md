# Information Security vs Cybersecurity

## What is Information Security?
**Information Security (InfoSec)** is the practice of protecting all forms of information—whether digital or physical—from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction.

### Key Focus Areas:
- Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA Triad)
- Protection of paper records, intellectual property, and verbal communications
- Broader than just digital assets

---

## What is Cybersecurity?
**Cybersecurity** is a subset of information security that specifically focuses on protecting digital systems, networks, and data from cyber threats.

### Key Focus Areas:
- Internet-connected systems
- Malware, ransomware, phishing, and hacking prevention
- Network security, application security, endpoint protection

---

## Difference Between Information Security and Cybersecurity

| Aspect              | Information Security                  | Cybersecurity                        |
|---------------------|----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Scope               | Broad – includes physical & digital   | Narrower – focused on digital assets |
| Focus               | Information in any form               | Protection of digital systems        |
| Examples            | Locking file cabinets, access control | Firewalls, antivirus, intrusion detection |
| Dependency          | Includes cybersecurity as a subset    | Part of the larger InfoSec umbrella  |

---

# What is Hacking?
**Hacking** is the act of gaining unauthorized access to data in a system or network, often exploiting vulnerabilities.

### Types of Hacking:
- System hacking
- Network hacking
- Social engineering

---

# What is Ethical Hacking?
**Ethical Hacking** involves legally breaking into systems and networks to test their security. Ethical hackers help organizations find and fix vulnerabilities.

### Purpose:
- Improve security
- Prevent real attacks
- Compliance testing

---

## Difference Between Hacking and Ethical Hacking

| Aspect        | Hacking                                | Ethical Hacking                      |
|---------------|----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Legality      | Illegal (unauthorized access)          | Legal (authorized testing)           |
| Intent        | Harmful (data theft, damage)           | Helpful (identify and fix flaws)     |
| Tools         | Same tools, different usage            | Used with permission and reporting   |

---

# Types of Hackers

## White Hat Hacker
- Ethical hacker
- Works with organizations
- Goal: Protect and secure systems

## Black Hat Hacker
- Malicious hacker
- Works independently or for crime groups
- Goal: Exploit systems for personal gain

---

## Difference Between White Hat and Black Hat Hackers

| Aspect           | White Hat Hacker                      | Black Hat Hacker                      |
|------------------|----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Intent           | Defensive – protect systems           | Offensive – exploit systems            |
| Legality         | Legal                                 | Illegal                                |
| Employment       | Security firms, government, companies | Underground networks                   |
| Methods Used     | Same techniques, with authorization   | Same techniques, without authorization |
