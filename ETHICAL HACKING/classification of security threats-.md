# Classification of Security Threats

Security threats can be classified based on their origin and target level. They include **Network-level**, **Host (Node)-level**, **Application-level**, **Physical**, **Human**, and **Natural** threats.

---

## 1. Network-Level Threats

These attacks target communication channels, data transmission, and network infrastructure.

**Examples:**
- **Denial of Service (DoS) / DDoS:** Overloading the network with fake traffic to make it unavailable.  
- **Sniffing / Eavesdropping:** Intercepting data packets to steal confidential information.  
- **Spoofing:** Pretending to be another device or user (e.g., IP spoofing, ARP spoofing).  
- **Man-in-the-Middle (MitM):** Intercepting and modifying data between two communicating parties.  
- **Routing Attacks:** Tampering with routing tables or network protocols.

---

## 2. Host (Node)-Level Threats

These target individual devices such as computers, servers, or IoT nodes.

**Examples:**
- **Malware:** Viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, spyware.  
- **Unauthorized Access:** Exploiting weak passwords or vulnerabilities.  
- **Privilege Escalation:** Gaining higher-level permissions than authorized.  
- **Data Theft or Alteration:** Stealing or tampering with sensitive data.  
- **Backdoors:** Hidden access points left by attackers or insiders.

---

## 3. Application-Level Threats

These exploit vulnerabilities in software or web applications.

**Examples:**
- **SQL Injection:** Injecting malicious database queries.  
- **Cross-Site Scripting (XSS):** Running malicious scripts in users’ browsers.  
- **Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF):** Forcing users to execute unwanted actions.  
- **Buffer Overflow:** Overwriting memory to execute arbitrary code.  
- **Session Hijacking:** Stealing user session cookies or tokens.

---

## 4. Physical Threats

These involve damage or loss of physical infrastructure or hardware.

**Examples:**
- **Hardware Theft:** Stealing computers, servers, or storage devices.  
- **Device Damage:** Intentional destruction or vandalism.  
- **Power Failure:** Interrupting power supply to systems.  
- **Fire or Water Damage:** Physical destruction of assets.

---

## 5. Human Threats

These arise from human actions—either intentional (malicious) or accidental.

**Examples:**
- **Social Engineering:** Manipulating people to reveal confidential information.  
- **Insider Threats:** Employees or trusted users misusing access.  
- **Negligence:** Unintentional errors, such as mishandling data or weak passwords.  
- **Phishing:** Deceptive emails or messages tricking users into sharing credentials.

---

## 6. Natural Threats

These result from environmental or natural events beyond human control.

**Examples:**
- **Earthquakes**  
- **Floods**  
- **Fires**  
- **Storms / Lightning**  
- **Pandemics or Biological Hazards**

---
