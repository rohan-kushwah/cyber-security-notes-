## **Security Threats and Threat Modeling in Cybersecurity**

---

### ⚠️ **1. What is a Security Threat?**

A **security threat** is any **potential danger or risk** that can harm a system, network, or data by exploiting vulnerabilities.  
These threats can cause **data loss, unauthorized access, or system damage**.

🧠 **In short:**

> A threat is anything that can **break the security** of your system.

---

### 💣 **2. Types of Security Threats**

#### 🔹 **a. Malware**

Malicious software like **viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware**, or **spyware** that can damage or steal data.

#### 🔹 **b. Phishing**

Fake emails or messages that **trick users** into revealing sensitive information.

#### 🔹 **c. Denial of Service (DoS/DDoS)**

Attackers **overload** a system or website, making it unavailable to real users.

#### 🔹 **d. Man-in-the-Middle (MitM)**

Attackers **intercept** communication between two parties to steal or alter data.

#### 🔹 **e. Insider Threat**

An **employee or trusted person** misuses access to harm the organization.

#### 🔹 **f. SQL Injection**

Hackers inject **malicious SQL commands** to access or manipulate databases.

#### 🔹 **g. Zero-Day Attack**

Exploits a **software vulnerability** before the vendor can fix it.

---

### 🧩 **3. What is Threat Modeling?**

**Threat Modeling** is a process used to **identify, analyze, and prioritize potential threats** to a system.  
It helps security teams understand **where and how attacks can happen**, and plan defenses in advance.

🧠 **In short:**

> Threat Modeling = **Thinking like a hacker** to protect the system better.

---

### 🧠 **4. Common Threat Modeling Approaches**

#### 🔹 **a. STRIDE Model (by Microsoft)**

Focuses on six types of threats:  
**S**poofing, **T**ampering, **R**epudiation, **I**nformation disclosure, **D**enial of service, **E**levation of privilege.

#### 🔹 **b. PASTA (Process for Attack Simulation and Threat Analysis)**

A 7-step, risk-based model focused on **business impact**.

#### 🔹 **c. DREAD**

Rates threats based on **Damage, Reproducibility, Exploitability, Affected users, and Discoverability**.

---

### 👨‍💻 **5. Types of Cybersecurity Teams**

#### 🔹 **a. Red Team (Attackers)**

- Act like **hackers** to test the system’s defense.
    
- Perform **penetration testing** and **ethical hacking**.
    

#### 🔹 **b. Blue Team (Defenders)**

- Protect and **defend** the organization’s network.
    
- Monitor systems, detect, and respond to attacks.
    

#### 🔹 **c. Purple Team (Collaborators)**

- **Bridge** between Red and Blue teams.
    
- Share insights to **improve both attack and defense strategies**.
