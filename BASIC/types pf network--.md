what is internet

The **Internet** is a global network of interconnected computers and servers that communicate using standardized protocols (like **TCP/IP**). It allows people all over the world to share information, access websites, send emails, watch videos, play games, and much more.

Here’s a simpler breakdown:

- 🌐 **Network of networks**: The internet connects millions of smaller networks (like your home Wi-Fi, your school or work network).
    
- 🧠 **Information highway**: It lets devices talk to each other, transfer data, and run services (like websites, apps, etc.).
    
- 📡 **Works via infrastructure**: This includes cables (fiber optics, undersea), routers, satellites, and data centers.
    

And to access it, you typically use an **Internet Service Provider (ISP)** like Comcast, AT&T, or Vodafone, depending on your region.

An **intranet** is like a private version of the internet that’s only accessible within a specific organization—think of it as the **internet, but for internal use only**.

Here’s a breakdown:

- 🏢 **Private network**: Used by companies, schools, or organizations to share internal information, tools, and resources.
    
- 🔐 **Access restricted**: Only employees or authorized users can log in—usually protected by passwords, firewalls, and other security measures.
    
- 📁 **Common uses**:
    
    - Sharing company news or announcements
        
    - Internal documents, forms, and policies
        
    - HR portals, project collaboration tools, etc.
        
- 💻 **Runs on web tech**: It often looks and works like a website, but it’s hosted on internal servers.
    

So, while the **internet** is public and global, the **intranet** is private and local to an organization.

There are several **types of networks**, classified based on size, range, and purpose. Here are the main ones:

---

### 🔹 1. **PAN (Personal Area Network)**

- **Range**: A few meters (like around your desk)
    
- **Example**: Connecting your phone to a Bluetooth headset or smartwatch.
    
- **Use**: Personal devices communication.
    

---

### 🔹 2. **LAN (Local Area Network)**

- **Range**: Covers a single building or campus.
    
- **Example**: Wi-Fi in your home, office, or school.
    
- **Use**: Sharing files, printers, and internet among devices in one place.
    

---

### 🔹 3. **MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)**

- **Range**: Spans a city or a large campus.
    
- **Example**: Network between different branches of a university in a city.
    
- **Use**: Connects multiple LANs in a region.
    

---

### 🔹 4. **WAN (Wide Area Network)**

- **Range**: Covers large areas—countries or continents.
    
- **Example**: The **Internet** itself is the biggest WAN.
    
- **Use**: Connects LANs and MANs over long distances.
    

---

### 🔹 5. **WLAN (Wireless LAN)**

- A wireless version of LAN using Wi-Fi.
    
- **Example**: Your home Wi-Fi network.
